Regional Digital Content Creator Economies Research Methodology

Research Scope and Ethical Safeguards

Geography
• **Primary Focus**: Nebraska and Iowa
• **Urban Anchors**: 
- Nebraska: Omaha, Lincoln (plus secondary areas: Grand Island, Kearney)
- Iowa: Des Moines, Cedar Rapids, Iowa City (plus micropolitan areas)


Population Definition
• Adult content creators operating publicly with identifiable ties to NE or IA
• Creator-run accounts, agency-managed pages, or collectives
• Active within last 90 days


Data Sources and Privacy Constraints
• **Allowed**: Publicly available profiles and pages
• **Prohibited**: 
- Gated or paywalled content
- Direct messages
- Private groups
- Leaked materials
- Personal identifiers (exact addresses, phone numbers, personal emails)


Ethical Use Framework
• **Purpose**: Academic market research and economic analysis
• **Prohibited Uses**: Targeting, harassment, disclosure of sensitive PII
• **Privacy by Design**: Anonymized IDs, no doxxing vectors
• **Public Business Information Only**: Report only business-facing information creators have made public


Sampling and Discovery Methodology

Creator Discovery Pipeline

Platform Search Strategy
1. **Primary Platforms**: X/Twitter, Instagram, TikTok, Reddit
2. **Creator Platforms**: Fansly, OnlyFans landing pages, ManyVids storefronts
3. **Link Hubs**: Linktree, Beacons, Carrd


Search Keywords and Hashtags

State Tags: #Omaha, #Lincoln, #DesMoines, #IowaCity, #NE, #IA
City Tags: #GrandIsland, #Kearney, #CedarRapids
Creator Terms: #midwestcreator, #OF, #Fansly, #clipstore
Combined: #OmahaCreator, #IowaAdultCreator, #MidwestOF


Network Expansion Protocol
1. Start with verified creators
2. Follow graph analysis for collaborators
3. Identify local photographers and studios
4. Map agency affiliations
5. Track event booking patterns


Inclusion Criteria Checklist
• Public account self-identifying as adult creator
• Active within last 90 days
• Demonstrable NE or IA connection
• Public business presence
• No violation of platform terms


Verification Framework

Signal Hierarchy (Scoring System)

High-Confidence Signals (Weight: 0.8-1.0)
• Registered business entity in state
• Press interviews naming city/state
• Event bookings with local venues
• Creator bio with consistent state references
• Business license verification


Medium-Confidence Signals (Weight: 0.5-0.7)
• Geo-tagged posts over 30+ days
• Local collaboration credits
• Repeated city name mentions
• Local photographer tags
• Regional event participation


Low-Confidence Signals (Weight: 0.2-0.4)
• Single state hashtag
• Sporadic location references
• Ambiguous posts
• Temporary location mentions


Verification Labels

creator_state: "NE", "IA", or "Undetermined"
confidence_score: [0.0, 1.0]
verification_level: "High", "Medium", "Low", "Undetermined"


Evidence Collection Protocol
1. **Screenshot Evidence**: Capture bio, location tags, business info
2. **URL Documentation**: Store direct links with timestamps
3. **Cross-Reference**: Verify across multiple platforms
4. **Audit Trail**: Maintain evidence excerpts for each signal
5. **Quality Control**: Regular verification audits


Data Processing and Quality Assurance

Data Hygiene Standards
• Rate limiting: 1 request per second per platform
• Respect robots.txt and platform API limits
• User-Agent rotation for web scraping
• Timestamp all data collections
• Store raw data with processing logs


Data Normalization Procedures
1. **Platform Standardization**: Consistent naming conventions
2. **Metric Harmonization**: Standardize likes/hearts/views
3. **Time Zone Unification**: Convert to CST/CDT
4. **Handle Standardization**: Remove @ symbols, normalize case
5. **URL Validation**: Ensure all links are accessible


Anonymization Protocol
1. **Creator ID Generation**: SHA-256 hash of unique identifier
2. **Name Replacement**: Replace with creator_id only
3. **Secure Crosswalk**: Store mapping separately with restricted access
4. **Data Minimization**: Collect only necessary business information
5. **Regular Purging**: Remove unnecessary personal data


Quality Control and Validation

Data Validation Checks
• Creator activity within 90 days
• Platform presence verification
• Location signal consistency
• Business information accuracy
• Duplicate detection and removal


Bias Mitigation Strategies
1. **Platform Diversity**: Ensure representation across platforms
2. **Geographic Balance**: Track urban vs rural representation
3. **Temporal Sampling**: Avoid time-based sampling bias
4. **Size Representation**: Include various creator sizes
5. **Content Type Balance**: Ensure diversity across content categories


Regular Audits
• Monthly data quality reviews
• Verification scoring calibration
• Privacy compliance audits
• Bias assessment reports
• Methodology updates


Compliance and Risk Management

Privacy Controls
• **PII Exclusion**: No personal addresses, phone numbers, personal emails
• **Business Information Only**: Public business emails and booking contacts
• **Data Minimization**: Collect only what's necessary for research
• **Secure Storage**: Encrypted data storage with access controls


Safety and Content Filters
• **Minor Detection**: Automated age verification checks
• **Content Compliance**: Platform terms of service alignment
• **Safety Protocols**: Exclude illegal or harmful content
• **Reporting Mechanisms**: Clear violation reporting procedures


Legal Compliance
• **Terms of Service**: Platform API and scraping compliance
• **State Regulations**: Nebraska and Iowa business record laws
• **Academic Standards**: IRB considerations for human subjects research
• **Data Protection**: GDPR-like privacy standards


Reporting and Dissemination

Output Formats
1. **Aggregate Statistics**: No individual creator identification
2. **Regional Analysis**: Metropolitan and micropolitan area reports
3. **Trend Analysis**: Time-series market dynamics
4. **Comparative Studies**: Nebraska vs Iowa market comparisons


Publication Guidelines
• **Anonymized Data Only**: Never include identifying information
• **Aggregate Focus**: Market-level insights and trends
• **Methodology Transparency**: Clear documentation of processes
• **Limitations Disclosure**: Honest assessment of research constraints

Documentation Requirements

All research activities must maintain:
1. **Data Collection Logs**: Timestamp and source for all data
2. **Verification Evidence**: Screenshots and URL documentation
3. **Processing Logs**: All data transformations and decisions
4. **Quality Control Records**: Validation results and corrections
5. **Compliance Documentation**: Privacy and legal compliance records


This methodology ensures rigorous, ethical, and privacy-protective research while providing valuable insights into the regional digital creator economies of Nebraska and Iowa.
